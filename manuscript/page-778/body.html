The pomp of rank, the assumption of power, the possessions of wealth vanished like morning mist. One living beggar had become of more worth than a national peerage of dead lords-- alas the day!--than of dead heroes, patriots, or men of genius. There was much of degradation in this: for even vice and virtue had lost their attributes--life--life--the continuation of our animal mechanism-- was the Alpha and Omega of the desires, the prayers, the prostrate ambition of human race.
